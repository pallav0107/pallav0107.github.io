The Jackpot Study

We mapped every option that could make you rich — 841,668 cheap tickets, 25 parameters each — to answer one question: is there a repeatable setup that predicts a jackpot, or is it all luck? The answer, honestly, is both.

Across eleven years, the ceiling of the possible is about ~33,000–49,000× on a single perfectly-caught deep-OTM call (TSLA, MSTR, SMCI, MU). 100,000× is off the map — not rare, nonexistent. And of 55,827 raw "heroes," only ~38 were realistically buyable from a tradeable entry. The dream is real but astronomically thin.

02 Where the jackpots hide (by quarter)

Heroes appear in every single quarter — the tail is a permanent feature, not a fluke. But the density swings 80× and clusters in volatility storms: COVID (2020), meme mania (2021), the AI/crypto parabola (2024).

Read the shape honestly. The typical alpha ticket still loses −88%, and only 11% win. The entire +358% comes from the rare tail. It's a genuine positive-EV edge — but only over many bets. Place five and you'll see the median (a near-total loss); place three hundred and the mean shows up.

05 The breakthrough it unlocked

The single biggest lever above — days-to-expiry — exposed a flaw in our live engine (the "Arena"): it held options only 21 days. The jackpot EV lives at 63–126 days. When we stretched the hold on the honest, robustness-corrected engine:

3–5× more money, with lower drawdown — and earned broadly across 9 of 10 years, not on luck. The engine had been fishing the wrong part of the term structure for a decade. That's the whole point of the study: not a bigger lottery ticket, but a repeatable, verified reason the tail pays — and where.

The one honest line. There's no setup that makes 100,000× — that ticket has never existed. But there is a real, repeatable situation — long-dated, deep-OTM, momentum — that turns a 1-in-411 lottery into a 1-in-32 one, pays ~9× the average, and, held long enough, quietly compounds. The jackpot isn't a number you pick. It's a place you stand.
